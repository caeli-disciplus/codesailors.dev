Why writing maintainable, readable code is just as important as making it work.

## The SOLID Principles

Single Responsibility, Open/Closed, Liskov Substitution, Interface Segregation, and Dependency Inversion — these five principles form the foundation of clean object-oriented design.

## Naming Things Well

Clear, intention-revealing names are worth more than any comment. If you need a comment to explain what a variable does, rename it.

## Refactoring Without Fear

Tests give you the confidence to refactor. Extract methods, rename variables, and restructure classes knowing that your test suite will catch regressions immediately.

## Design Patterns

Patterns like Factory, Observer, and Strategy are a shared vocabulary for communicating design intent — use them when they clarify, not to show off.
